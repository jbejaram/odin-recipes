Sourdough Discard Tortillas Recipe

Source: Wikimedia Commons

Description

Soft and flavorful tortillas made with sourdough discard, offering a subtle tang and chewy texture — perfect for tacos, wraps, or as a homemade bread alternative.

Ingredients:

  • 90 g sourdough discard (unfed)
  • 60 g all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (or any neutral oil)
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1–2 tbsp water, as needed
  • (Optional: a pinch of garlic powder, oregano, or sesame seeds for flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Mix the dry ingredients (flour and salt) in a bowl.
  2. Add the discard and oil. Stir with a spoon or your hands.
  3. Add water gradually until the dough comes together — it should be soft but not sticky. If your discard is very liquid, use less water; if it’s thick, add a bit more.
  4. Knead for about 3–5 minutes until smooth.
  5. Rest the dough for 20 minutes, covered, to relax the gluten.
  6. Divide into 4 small balls and roll out each one with a rolling pin until thin (about 2–3 mm).
  7. Cook on a hot dry skillet or griddle for 1–2 minutes per side, until bubbles appear and brown spots form.
